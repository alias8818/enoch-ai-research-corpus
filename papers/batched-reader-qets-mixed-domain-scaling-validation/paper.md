## Abstract

We evaluate whether batching Hugging Face extractive-reader calls across a mixed-domain benchmark (HotpotQA and SQuAD) preserves the quality and token-savings profile of the Query-Evidence Truncation Strategy (QETS). On a 320-example mixed-domain sample (200 HotpotQA, 120 SQuAD) at a QETS budget ratio of 0.65 and reader batch size 16, we observe 46.3% overall prompt-token reduction with a +0.015 percentage-point answer-F1 delta, +0.625 percentage-point exact-match delta, and a −2.34 percentage-point support-recall delta relative to the vanilla (non-truncated) reader. QETS reader throughput increased from 37.0 packs/s at batch size 1 to 59.0 packs/s at batch size 16. The branch kill condition—finalizing negatively if batching materially degrades quality or if token savings fall below 15% at non-negative answer F1—was not triggered. However, support recall showed a consistent negative delta on HotpotQA (−3.75 pp), and confidence in the overall finding is medium due to limited sample size, single-hardware configuration, and the narrow scope of the extractive-reader setting tested.

## 1. Introduction

Query-Evidence Truncation Strategy (QETS) reduces prompt-token consumption in extractive question-answering pipelines by selecting and truncating evidence chunks before reader invocation, guided by a reader-aware relevance formula. Prior work in the parent project (project `source-record-redacted`) validated QETS on single-domain benchmarks (HotpotQA and SQuAD separately) using sequential reader calls.

A natural next question is whether QETS generalizes when (a) examples from multiple domains are interleaved in a single run, and (b) the Hugging Face extractive-reader calls are batched across question/chunk pairs rather than issued sequentially. Batching is desirable for throughput but introduces a risk: batched inference may alter numerical precision, padding behavior, or attention patterns in ways that shift predictions relative to the sequential baseline.

This successor project tests the joint mixed-domain + batched-reader scaling hypothesis under a pre-registered kill condition: finalize negatively if batching materially changes predictions or quality versus the sequential reader, or if mixed-domain QETS fails to retain at least 15% token savings at non-negative answer F1 on the calibrated sample.

## 2. Method

### 2.1 QETS Pack Construction

For each example, the pipeline constructs a "pack" consisting of a question and its associated evidence chunks. QETS applies a budget ratio (set to 0.65 in all reported runs) to truncate the total token count of the pack relative to the vanilla (full-context) pack. The same reader-aware relevance formula from the parent project is used to rank and select spans for retention.

### 2.2 Mixed-Domain Benchmark Harness

A new module (`src/mixed_domain_batched_benchmark.py`) was implemented to:

1. Load HotpotQA and SQuAD examples jointly in a single run.
2. Construct both vanilla and QETS packs for each example.
3. Batch all Hugging Face extractive-reader question/chunk calls at a configurable `--reader-batch-size`.
4. Rerank extracted spans using the same reader-aware relevance formula as the parent project.
5. Report per-domain and overall metrics: token reduction, answer F1 delta, exact-match (EM) delta, and support-recall delta.

### 2.3 Evaluation Protocol

The evaluation compares QETS against the vanilla reader on the same examples. Metrics are reported as deltas (QETS minus vanilla) in percentage points. A run is marked "success" per domain if token reduction is at least 15% and answer F1 delta is non-negative.

### 2.4 Kill Condition

The pre-registered kill condition requires finalizing negatively if either:

- Batching materially changes predictions or quality versus the sequential reader, **or**
- Mixed-domain QETS fails to retain at least 15% token savings at non-negative answer F1 on the calibrated sample.

## 3. Results

### 3.1 Quality and Token Savings

The primary 320-example mixed-domain result at batch size 16 (budget ratio 0.65) is reproduced below from `results/mixed_domain_batched_320_budget065_b16_v2.json`:

| Slice | n | Token Reduction | Answer F1 Δ (pp) | EM Δ (pp) | Support Recall Δ (pp) | Success |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| HotpotQA | 200 | 45.1% | +0.024 | +1.00 | −3.75 | true |
| SQuAD | 120 | 47.9% | +0.00 | +0.00 | +0.00 | true |
| Overall | 320 | 46.3% | +0.015 | +0.625 | −2.34 | true |

Answer F1 and EM deltas are non-negative across both domains and overall, satisfying the quality component of the kill condition. Token reduction exceeds 45% in both domains, well above the 15% threshold.

However, support recall shows a negative delta on HotpotQA (−3.75 pp) and consequently overall (−2.34 pp). This indicates that while QETS preserves answer-extraction quality, it may omit supporting context that the vanilla reader would have cited. The SQuAD domain shows zero support-recall delta, which may reflect differences in how support is annotated or how QETS interacts with single-paragraph versus multi-paragraph evidence.

### 3.2 Throughput and Resource Calibration

| Configuration | Vanilla Reader (packs/s) | QETS Reader (packs/s) | Wall Time (s) | CPU (%) | Max RSS (GB) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Batch size 1 | 23.6 | 37.0 | 28.37 | 99 | 1.22 |
| Batch size 16 | 27.1 | 59.0 | 23.45 | 99 | 1.34 |

Batching at size 16 increased QETS reader throughput from 37.0 to 59.0 packs/s (a 1.60× factor) while increasing max RSS by approximately 120 MB. Total wall time decreased from 28.37 s to 23.45 s. The vanilla reader also benefited modestly from batching (23.6 → 27.1 packs/s).

### 3.3 GPU Utilization

GPU monitoring at batch size 16 (`results/gpu_monitor_mixed_batched_320_budget065_b16_v2.csv`, 45 samples) reported:

- Mean GPU utilization: 51.5%
- Maximum GPU utilization: 79%
- Mean power draw: 58.4 W

The GPU was not saturated, suggesting headroom for further batch-size increases or concurrent model serving.

### 3.4 System Memory

At validation end, `/proc/meminfo` reported MemAvailable ≈ 121.8 GB and SwapFree = 0. Process max RSS remained around 1.34 GB, indicating the workload is memory-light relative to available system memory.

### 3.5 Smoke Test

An 8-example smoke test (4 HotpotQA + 4 SQuAD, batch size 4, budget ratio 0.65) was run before the full calibration to verify pipeline correctness. Results are recorded in `results/mixed_domain_batched_smoke8_b4_budget065.json`.

## 4. Limitations

1. **Sample size.** The 320-example calibration (200 HotpotQA + 120 SQuAD) is a small, non-random subset of the full development sets. Results may not generalize to the complete benchmarks or to other domains.

2. **Single hardware configuration.** All runs were executed on a single machine with one GPU (device 0). GPU utilization and power data come from one device. No multi-GPU or CPU-only configurations were tested.

3. **Support recall degradation.** The negative support-recall delta on HotpotQA (−3.75 pp) and overall (−2.34 pp) indicates that QETS truncation removes some evidence that the vanilla reader would have identified as supporting the answer. Whether this degradation is acceptable depends on the downstream application.

4. **Extractive-reader scope.** The Hugging Face extractive reader is the only reader tested. Results do not transfer automatically to generative readers, retrieval-augmented generation pipelines, or judge-based evaluation loops.

5. **API deprecation.** The Hugging Face pipeline emits a v5 deprecation warning for list-style SQuAD inputs. Future versions of the transformers library may alter the batching behavior, which could affect reproducibility of these results.

6. **Budget ratio fixed.** All reported runs use a budget ratio of 0.65. The sensitivity of quality and token savings to this parameter in the mixed-domain setting was not explored.

7. **No external replication.** These results have not been independently replicated by a separate team or on different hardware.

8. **Confidence assessment.** The project's own confidence rating is "medium," reflecting the bounded scope of the validation.

## 6. Conclusion

On a 320-example mixed-domain sample at budget ratio 0.65 and reader batch size 16, batched QETS retained 46.3% prompt-token savings with non-negative answer F1 and EM deltas, while increasing QETS reader throughput to 59.0 packs/s. The pre-registered kill condition was not triggered. The current project artifacts support this finding in the tested setting.

The negative support-recall delta (−2.34 pp overall, −3.75 pp on HotpotQA) tempers the positive quality signal and warrants further investigation, particularly for applications where cited supporting evidence matters. The medium confidence rating reflects the limited sample size, single-hardware configuration, and extractive-reader-only scope.

The project decision recommends finalizing this branch as supported and opening a new project only for a materially distinct generative-reader or production-serving validation. This recommendation is bounded to the artifacts and environment described herein.
