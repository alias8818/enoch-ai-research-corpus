## Abstract

This report evaluates whether a segment-level context firewall can preserve useful web-agent task context while reducing prompt-injection susceptibility for a capable local planner. The validation uses a locally provisioned Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct GGUF planner on realistic shopping, travel, and billing extraction tasks. The supported result is evidence-bound: on this 60-case validation, full context solved clean tasks but remained susceptible to contaminated-page injections, while the segment firewall eliminated observed injections without clean-task regression.

## Claim Scope

The project decision is `finalize_positive` with hypothesis status `supported`, confidence `high`, and evidence strength `strong`. ## Method

The branch acquired the parent segment-firewall implementation and added `experiments/realistic_model_planner_benchmark.py`, a live OpenAI-compatible planner benchmark over realistic shopping, travel, and billing cases. After endpoint probing found no already-running compatible endpoint, the run provisioned a local llama.cpp service for `Qwen/Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct-GGUF` and then stopped the helper service after measurement.

The validation gate required high clean task success, nonzero full-context injection susceptibility, at least 40% firewall attack reduction, and less than 5% clean regression. A cached Phi-4-mini negative-control attempt failed to qualify as a strong enough planner, preserving the concern that weak local models may not be suitable for this validation.
