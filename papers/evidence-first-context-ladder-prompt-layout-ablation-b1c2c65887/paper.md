- - Deterministic repo-QA control: `results/ablation_deterministic/repo_qa_summary.md`; all ladder layouts matched exactly on evidence recall/F1 because selection is unchanged and the extractive reader is order-insensitive.
- - Local LLM ablation: `results/ablation_llm/repo_qa_llm_results.json` and `results/ablation_analysis.md` using Qwen/Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, seed 344, 12 repo-QA items, budgets 600/900/1200.
- - Evidence-first vs summary-leading generated-answer F1 deltas: +0.299 at budget 600, +0.060 at 900, +0.226 at 1200. Wins/ties/losses across budget-query pairs: 14/17/5.
- - Summary-demoted was mixed: +0.154 at 600, -0.089 at 900, +0.175 at 1200; wins/ties/losses 14/13/9.
- - Kill condition not met: evidence-first improved at least one repo-QA item and improved mean F1 at all tested budgets without changing token count or evidence recall.
- - Performance note: full LLM run took ~200.1s for 144 generations after cached model load. Pre/post UMA memory showed ~116 GiB available; idle GPU samples were 0% utilization, so sampled utilization is too coarse to prove saturation.
- Conclusion: evidence-first ordering is the branch-specific mechanism to carry forward; summary-demotion prose is less reliable and should not be the default.
