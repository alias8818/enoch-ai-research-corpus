- - Smoke: `results/real_repo_qwen7b_smoke` completed 4 cases / 24 live answer trials against `<local-path-redacted>` with Qwen2.5-7B GGUF and confirmed server/run cleanup.
- - Atomic real-repo run: `results/real_repo_qwen7b_reduced_planner` completed 24 cases across 8 source-file groups and 288 answer trials. The amortized 6-token planner matched vanilla 190-token and 260-token baselines with feedback budget 90, all-in prompt-like deltas -38.6% and -58.2% respectively; per-question planning also met the >=15% all-in savings gate on those baselines. At the tight 90/130 baselines, fixed planner overhead still prevented all-in savings despite equal or near-equal quality.
- - Hard paired-evidence run: `results/real_repo_qwen7b_pair_hard` completed 16 paired cases across 8 groups and 192 answer trials, requiring two source facts per answer. Amortized feedback improved equal-budget F1 by +24.7 points at 90 tokens and +10.2 points at 130 tokens, with evidence-recall gains of +62.5 and +65.6 points. Cost-normalized matches met the success gate: vanilla 190 F1 0.953 matched by amortized feedback 90 F1 0.969 with -18.9% all-in prompt-like tokens; vanilla 260 F1 0.974 matched by amortized feedback 130 F1 0.984 with -31.0% all-in tokens. Per-question feedback also met the gate for 190/260 baselines, though amortization was cheaper.
- - Performance/utilization: Qwen2.5-7B live answer calls averaged roughly 39-42 completion tok/s. Atomic run CPU mean ~5.56%, GPU mean ~95.8%, RSS ~0.75-2.07 GB, MemAvailable after run 115.6 GB. Pair run CPU mean ~5.55%, GPU mean ~95.7%, RSS ~0.76-1.81 GB, MemAvailable after run 115.9 GB. No helper `llama-server` process remained after completion.
- - Interpretation: the reduced query-only/6-token hidden planner survived a larger real-repository validation and the branch-specific kill condition is not supported. The effect is strongest when vanilla needs larger context to recover multiple source facts and when the first pass is amortized across related questions; tiny baselines remain dominated by fixed planner overhead, and saturated single-fact cases are less informative even though they show cost savings against larger vanilla contexts.
